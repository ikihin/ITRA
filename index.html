The insight

Domestic is solved

MoMo and VNPay already win for VND, QR, and local commerce. Competing there is a trap — and the wrong “why onchain.”

Cross-border is broken for small invoices

SWIFT and correspondent banking optimize large wires. Marketplaces optimize closed platforms. Neither optimizes a $800 design milestone paid from Berlin to Bình Thạnh.

Stablecoins fix the middle mile

USDC on Solana gives global reach, minute settlement, and a public receipt. Off-ramp to VND stays a licensed Web2 partner problem — we don’t pretend to be a bank.

Who it’s for — and who it isn’t

Primary ICP

  • Digital agencies in VN (design, dev, content, marketing) with recurring overseas clients
  • Senior freelancers billing $3k+/month outside marketplaces
  • Invoice size mostly $200–$5,000, paid monthly/milestone
  • Already comfortable with English clients; pain is rails, not finding work

Not the focus (yet)

  • Retail remittance / “send money to family”
  • Domestic P2P or coffee-shop QR (MoMo wins)
  • Crypto trading, yield, or token launches
  • Enterprises that already have bank treasury teams

★ SEN 01 · Business today & why onchain

The business today is services export. The product is settlement.

Vietnam’s freelancers and agencies already sell globally. The broken piece is not demand — it is how $200–$5,000 invoices cross borders.

Business today

Who: Design/dev/content agencies & freelancers in HCMC, Hanoi, Đà Nẵng paid by US/EU/SG/AU clients.

Current stack: Upwork · Payoneer · Wise · bank wire · marketplace holds.

Felt pain: Client sends ~$1,000 → often ~$920–950 lands after days. Fee stacking is opaque. Multi-person agency splits mean the pain multiplies every month.

Why it matters: Services export is real GDP for operators who will never get a factory LOAN — margin is everything.

Why onchain (and when it isn’t)

Yes: small, frequent, global settlement; public receipt; programmable split to teammates.

No: replacing MoMo for domestic VND; “crypto because cool”; taking retail deposits.

Mechanism: USDC on Solana = dollar stability + minute finality + low network cost.

Doubt we name: client onboarding to wallets, and VND off-ramp liquidity. Mitigate with card-on-ramp later + licensed partners — not fake claims.

Bounty submission notes

One-liner

SEN is the middle-mile settlement rail for Vietnam freelancers and agencies exporting services: clients pay USDC on Solana; teams settle in minutes with on-chain receipts — without replacing MoMo/VNPay for domestic VND.

1. The business today

Vietnam freelancers and small digital agencies already sell to US/EU/SG/AU clients. Money still moves through marketplaces, Payoneer/Wise, or banks. In the $200–$5,000 invoice band, stacked fees and multi-day delays routinely erase 5–10% of revenue — and agencies feel it on every cycle when splitting to teammates.

2. Why onchain

Domestic VND is solved. Onchain is justified only for small, frequent, cross-border settlement: global reach, minute finality, low network cost, and immutable receipts. Limits we accept: client wallet friction, VND off-ramp, tax, and contracts remain hybrid Web2. We are not building crypto MoMo.

3. How it works

Invoice-first UX → Solana Pay / USDC → settlement + receipt → optional agency batch split → optional licensed off-ramp. Product layers: invoices, team treasury, exports for books, partner cash-out. Demo: https://ikihin.github.io/SenPay/#demo

4. What’s next

15 operator interviews and 3 design partners → real Solana pay links → closed beta with agencies (USDC-in) → off-ramp LOI + tax export. North star: repeat invoices per agency per month, not vanity TVL. Regulatory posture: services-export settlement tool, not deposit-taking e-wallet.
